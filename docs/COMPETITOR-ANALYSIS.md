## 1. Direct Competitors

### 1.1 Zoho Workerly

- **Website**: [zoho.com/workerly](https://www.zoho.com/workerly/)
- **What they do**: End-to-end temp lifecycle management - scheduling, e-timesheets, invoicing, candidate matching
- **Pricing**:
  - ~$1/temp/month model
  - Starting from ~$45/month
  - Enterprise: $99/month
  - Free trial available
- **Strengths**:
  - Part of Zoho ecosystem (integrates with Zoho Books, CRM)
  - AI-powered candidate matching
  - Mobile apps for temps
  - Established brand with trust
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Complex UI (typical Zoho learning curve)
  - May be overkill for small agencies just needing scheduling
  - Pricing can add up with many temps
  - Focus on recruitment pipeline, not pure operations

### 1.2 Workstaff

- **Website**: [workstaff.app](https://workstaff.app)
- **What they do**: Workforce scheduling, time tracking, and staff management for flexible/temp workers
- **Pricing**:
  - Pro: $99/month per manager
  - Max: $139/month per manager (includes CRM, budgeting)
  - Enterprise: Custom
  - 14-day free trial
- **Strengths**:
  - Purpose-built for temp/flexible staff
  - Targets cleaning, hospitality, events specifically
  - Pay per manager, not per worker (good for large pools)
  - Client portal included
- **Weaknesses**:
  - $99-139/month may be expensive for 1-2 person operations
  - No Polish language support (English-focused)
  - Limited localization for European markets

### 1.3 Ubeya

- **Website**: [ubeya.com](https://www.ubeya.com/)
- **What they do**: AI-powered workforce management for staffing agencies, events, hospitality
- **Pricing**: Custom quotes (contact sales) - typically enterprise-focused
- **Strengths**:
  - AI shift scheduling and smart matching
  - Strong analytics (17% cost reduction reported)
  - Serves hospitality, events, stadiums - similar verticals
  - High ratings (4.6/5 Capterra, 4.8/5 G2)
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Enterprise-focused pricing (likely too expensive for small agencies)
  - Complex feature set may overwhelm simple use cases
  - No transparent pricing = sales process required

### 1.4 Shiftboard (ScheduleFlex)

- **Website**: [shiftboard.com](https://www.shiftboard.com/employee-scheduling-software/scheduleflex/staffing/)
- **What they do**: Shift scheduling with rules engine for staffing agencies
- **Pricing**: Custom/enterprise (no public pricing)
- **Strengths**:
  - Configurable rules engine for auto-assignment
  - Mobile app for workers
  - Mature platform with many integrations
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Enterprise-oriented, likely expensive
  - Heavy feature set for simple needs
  - US-market focused

### 1.5 Hyre

- **Website**: [hyrestaff.com](https://www.hyrestaff.com/)
- **What they do**: Workforce management with time clock and temp staff scheduling
- **Pricing**: Not publicly disclosed
- **Strengths**:
  - Simple scheduling interface
  - Self-service shift picking for workers
  - Time clock built-in
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Less established than major competitors
  - Limited information on feature depth

## 2. Indirect Alternatives (How People Solve This Today)

### 2.1 Excel/Google Sheets + Email

- **How it works**: Manual spreadsheets for worker database, schedules, and hours tracking. Communication via email/phone.
- **Why used**: Free, familiar, flexible, no learning curve
- **Pain points**:
  - No single source of truth
  - Version control nightmares
  - Time-consuming manual updates
  - Error-prone (copy-paste mistakes)
  - No audit trail
  - Doesn't scale past ~50 workers

### 2.2 WhatsApp/Messenger Groups + Calendar

- **How it works**: Group chats for availability and assignments, shared calendars for scheduling
- **Why used**: Already on phones, instant communication, free
- **Pain points**:
  - Messages get lost in chat history
  - No structured data (can't filter by skills/availability)
  - No hours tracking or reporting
  - Unprofessional for client-facing operations

### 2.3 Generic HR/Scheduling Tools (When I Work, Deputy, Homebase)

- **How it works**: General employee scheduling tools adapted for temp staffing
- **Why used**: Affordable ($2-4/user/month), good mobile apps
- **Pain points**:
  - Built for single-employer model, not agency model
  - No client/location management
  - No multi-tenant architecture
  - Missing temp-specific features (assignment lifecycle, billing reports)

## 3. Our Differentiation Opportunities

### 3.1 What We Could Do Better/Different

**Simplicity First**

- Strip away recruitment/ATS features - focus purely on operations
- Designed for coordinators, not HR professionals
- Minimal learning curve - should feel like "better Excel"

**Pricing for Small Agencies**

- Competitors: $99-139/month minimum or per-user fees
- Our target: 50-200 PLN/month (~$12-50)
- Makes professional tooling accessible to 50-150 worker agencies

**Local Market Focus (Poland/CEE)**

- Polish language interface
- Local payment methods
- Compliance with Polish labor regulations
- Support in local timezone/language

**Core Workflow Focus**

- Worker database with status tracking
- Simple scheduling board (not complex Gantt charts)
- Hours reporting for payroll/billing (Excel export)
- Audit log for accountability
- That's it. No bells and whistles.

### 3.2 Why We'd Be Unique

| Factor | Competitors | One Staff Dashboard |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Target** | Global, enterprise-first | Polish SMB agencies |
| **Price** | $99-139+/month | 50-200 PLN/month |
| **Complexity** | Full ATS + CRM + Payroll | Scheduling + Reporting only |
| **Positioning** | "Replace your entire HR stack" | "Replace your Excel sheets" |
| **Language** | English-first | Polish-first |

**Unique Value Proposition**:
> "The affordable, simple scheduling tool built specifically for Polish temp staffing agencies who've outgrown Excel but don't need enterprise software."

## 4. Key Takeaways for Presentation

- **Market exists**: Multiple well-funded competitors validate the problem
- **Gap identified**: No affordable, simple solution for small Polish agencies
- **Competitors are enterprise-focused**: Minimum $99/month, complex features
- **Current alternatives are painful**: Excel doesn't scale, generic tools don't fit
- **Our angle**: Simplicity + affordability + local market focus
